Benefits of doing business in UAE

The Emirates is one of the friendliest countries for entrepreneurs. It is a popular destination for foreigners for many reasons, including:

  • no income tax;
  • low import duties – from 0 to 5%;
  • strong and competitive economy;
  • support for start-ups as the UAE is home to many business incubators;
  • world-class infrastructure.

In addition, the UAE boasts an excellent quality of life ranking 21st in the Best Countries Index 2022.

Setting up a business in Dubai is the most popular option for the following reasons:

  • A growing and diverse economy. In terms of economic performance, Dubai has been the most successful of the emirates for many years.
  • Solid legal framework. In the UAE and Dubai, regulations are constantly being improved to encourage foreign investment.
  • Well-designed infrastructure. Dubai dominates the freight and re-export markets with the development of two ports, Port Rashid and Port of Jebel Ali.
  • Available labor resources. Thanks to the liberal labor policy in Dubai, it is easy and profitable to hire employees from almost any part of the world.
  • Free zones. There are more than 20 free zones in Dubai where foreign investors can open a business with 100% ownership and many other benefits.

Business in Dubai and UAE

Dubai and the UAE offer excellent conditions for large corporations, medium-sized businesses as well as small private companies. It only charges a 5% value-added tax for mainland companies, while businesses in the Free Trade Zones are not subject to VAT if their income does not exceed 375,000 AED ($102,000) per year.

Currently, companies in the UAE are not subject to corporate income tax, but from June 2023 it will be 9% for companies with an annual income of 375,000 AED ($102,000). If your taxable income is lower, you are exempt from this tax. These changes will only affect mainland companies and those free zone companies that operate exclusively in the UAE.

To do business in Dubai, it is not necessary to have a UAE resident visa, but obtaining one is easy: just apply for a residence permit on behalf of your company. To open any account at a local bank, other than a savings account, you will need a resident visa.

The speed and cost of registering a business in Dubai for a foreigner depends on the type of activity, the location of a company, as well as on visa permits. You can always check the current prices and terms with our specialists.
